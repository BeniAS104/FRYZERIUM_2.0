The Ultimate Guide to Choosing the Right Conditioner

Last updated: May 20, 2024 | 11 minutes read

You might think that any conditioner will make your hair soft and shiny, but it's not that simple. Just as there are many types of shampoos, there are also numerous conditioners. Choosing the wrong one can weigh your hair down or make it static, dry, and brittle. The right conditioner, however, can replenish moisture lost from shampooing, smooth the cuticle, reduce tangling, increase shine, decrease static, and make your hair more manageable.

Conditioner is essential for all hair types, but not every conditioner suits every hair type. We consulted experts - board-certified dermatologist Rachel Nazarian, MD, and celebrity hairstylist DJ Quintero - to guide you in selecting the right conditioner based on your hair type. Read on for their advice.

Understanding Conditioner Types

Conditioner packaging can be confusing with terms like "fortifying" and "volumizing." Here's a breakdown of what these terms mean:

  • Hydrating/Moisturizing: Ideal for thick, curly, or coarse hair, these conditioners add moisture, shine, and smoothness. Look for ceramides, B5, and triglycerides.
  • Volumizing: Best for fine or limp hair, these conditioners add lift without weighing hair down. Ingredients may include maltodextrin.
  • Strengthening/Fortifying: Formulated for damaged, over-processed, or brittle hair, these conditioners often contain amino acids, proteins, collagen, Olaplex technology, and biotin.
  • Balancing: A middle-ground option that neither overly moisturizes nor dries out the hair.
  • Smoothing/Straight Hair: These conditioners, which often contain cocoa butter, olive oil, and egg white, help seal the cuticle for a smooth hairstyle.
  • Curly Hair: Designed to be very moisturizing and reduce frizz, often featuring argan oil.

Best Conditioner for Weak, Breakage-Prone Hair

What to Look For:

Strengthening conditioners with ingredients like keratin and moisturizing agents. Green tea extract and other antioxidants are also beneficial.

Expert Tip:

Choose a reparative conditioner with nourishing ingredients that deeply condition the hair, such as Living Proof's Restore Conditioner or Nexxus Unbreakable Care Thickening Conditioner.

How to Use:

Avoid overusing protein-based products to prevent additional breakage.

Best Conditioner for Oily, Limp Hair

What to Look For:

Lightweight conditioners that add fullness and volume without weighing hair down. Ingredients like dimethicone, glycerin, and shea butter are ideal.

Expert Tip:

Avoid conditioners labeled as "hydrating" or "moisturizing." Opt for ones labeled "volumizing," "light," "strengthening," or "balancing."

How to Use:

Apply conditioner to the midshaft and ends of your hair, avoiding the scalp, and rinse thoroughly.

Best Conditioner for Dry, Damaged Hair

What to Look For:

Conditioners with vitamin B5 and ceramides to hydrate and improve moisture retention.

Expert Tip:

Use conditioners that seal cuticles and add shine and moisture, such as Living Proof's Perfect Hair Day Conditioner or No Frizz Conditioner.

How to Use:

Deep conditioners are effective when used one to four times a month. Coconut oil treatments can also help with intense damage repair.

Best Conditioner for Natural Curls and Coils

What to Look For:

Hydrating, moisturizing, and smoothing conditioners with ingredients like coconut and argan oils.

Expert Tip:

Use minimal but quality hair products, focusing on moisturizing conditioners, leave-in treatments, and oils. Co-washes can help maintain moisture without stripping natural oils.

How to Use:

Deep condition every two weeks to maintain moisture and protect your curls.

Can You Skip Conditioner?

For very fine hair, you might skip conditioner, but modern conditioner technology can cater to your specific hair needs. Consulting a board-certified dermatologist can help you choose the right products and address any underlying hair issues.
